Audio Processing

Apply server-side FFmpeg audio filters before pushing to platforms. Reduces background noise, room rumble, and uneven volume levels.

Enable Audio Processing Re-encode audio with noise reduction & compression
25 dB
Higher = more aggressive removal. Recommended: 20–30 dB for typical home/office noise.
Filter chain applied:
  • highpass=f=80 – Remove low-frequency rumble (< 80 Hz)
  • afftdn – FFT-based stationary noise suppression
  • agate – Noise gate (mutes near-silence passages)
  • acompressor – Dynamic compression (even loudness)
  • alimiter – Peak limiter (prevents clipping)
Audio will be re-encoded to AAC 160 kbps / 44.1 kHz instead of copied. Echo from your room should be handled in OBS (Noise Suppression filter).

Video Quality

Source passes through exactly what OBS sends — best quality, zero CPU. Other presets re-encode to a fixed resolution/bitrate; useful when your OBS bitrate exceeds a platform's cap.

Platform bitrate caps:
  • Twitch : 6,000 kbps (Partners)  /  3,500 kbps (Others)
  • Kick : ~12,000 kbps
  • YouTube : up to 51,000 kbps
